Scenario Guide
S1
Base Case
Both partners work until the portfolio hits the FIRE number. No disruptions. The benchmark all other scenarios are compared against.
S2
Sequential FIRE
Person A retires first; Person B works 3 more years. The remaining income covers expenses and lets the portfolio grow untouched.
S4
Lean FIRE
Retirement spend cut by 30%. Frugal lifestyle — fewer vacations, lower discretionary. Dramatically lowers the FIRE number and timeline.
S5
Fat FIRE
Targets 150% of retirement spend (min $150K/yr). Affluent retirement with travel and lifestyle upgrades. Larger portfolio and longer runway required.
S6
Sequence-of-Returns Risk
35% market crash in year 1 of retirement. Worst-case timing. If the portfolio still survives to life expectancy here, the plan is robust.
S7
Coast FIRE
Can you stop contributing now and coast to FIRE purely via compounding? If yes, you could downshift to a lower-stress job today.
S8
Geographic Arbitrage
Applies the COL reduction from Expenses tab to retirement spend. Models moving to a lower-cost city, state, or country (Portugal, Mexico, SE Asia…).
S9
Mortgage Paid Off
Retire mortgage-free. Reduces retirement expenses by the interest portion of the monthly payment. Compare vs S1 to judge payoff vs invest tradeoff.
S10
Layoff Disruption
Person A loses job for 12 months in year 1 — zero income, zero RSU vesting. Shows how a single tech layoff shifts the FIRE date.
